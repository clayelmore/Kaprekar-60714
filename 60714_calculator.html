The Number 60714

A universal attractor at every digit length d ≥ 5

Pick any number with at least five digits. Apply the right rule. Watch it converge to 60714.

The rule depends on the input's digit length — there is one rule at d = 5, another at d = 6, and a uniform recipe extending to every higher dimension. Each rule sorts the digits in two ways and combines them with a fixed coefficient vector. The result, after a finite number of iterations, is always 60714.

Try it
5 digits or more. Repdigits (like 11111) collapse to zero — try anything else.

The number 60714 is what the paper calls a dimension-transcendent attractor: a fixed point of a permutation-pair rule whose universality persists across every digit length d ≥ 5 simultaneously, not just at one specific d.

This is a strict generalization of Kaprekar's classical 1949 result. Kaprekar's constant 6174 is universal at d = 4 alone — the trick "stops working" at d = 5 if you keep his original rule fixed. The result extended here is that if you let the rule itself vary with d (specifically, by appending pairs of coefficients that sum to zero at each new digit length), there is a distinguished fixed point — 60714, sharing 6174's digit multiset plus a zero — whose universality holds at every d simultaneously, with an inductive proof that closes through both finite-state verification and a d-independent algebraic argument.

Full paper, the proof, the 506-fixed-point classification at d = 6, and reproducibility scripts: github.com/clayelmore/Kaprekar-60714
