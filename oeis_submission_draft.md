## %S — Sequence A_NEW (paired version, one per d on the 60714 ladder)

This is the sequence as it appears in the paper, with terms paired across the
two ladders (odd d=7,9,11,...; even d=8,10,12,...).

```
%I A_NEW
%S A_NEW 45,45,210,210,705,705,1992,1992,4995,4995,11430,11430,24300,24300
%N A_NEW |E_d^(1)|: number of step-1 collapse inputs at digit length d for the
generalized Kaprekar lifting of 60714, for d = 7, 8, 9, 10, ...
%C A_NEW E_d^(1) is the set of admissible inputs at digit length d whose
sorted-descending form has all entries beyond position d_F-1 = 4 equal, modulo
zero padding; equivalently, the inputs whose K_60714^(d) image equals 0 in one
step.
%C A_NEW Sequence is paired: a(d) = a(d+1) for odd d, reflecting the
two-ladder (odd, even) structure of the 60714 lifting.
%F A_NEW a(d) = C(floor((d-3)/2) + 9, 9) - 10 for d >= 7. Equivalently,
a(d) = binomial(k+9, 9) - 10 where k = floor((d-3)/2).
%H A_NEW C. Elmore, <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/PENDING">Universal Full-Variable
Fixed Points Under Coefficient-Preserving Liftings</a>, arXiv preprint, 2026.
%e A_NEW At d = 7: |E_7^(1)| = C(2+9, 9) - 10 = C(11, 9) - 10 = 55 - 10 = 45.
%e A_NEW At d = 11: |E_11^(1)| = C(4+9, 9) - 10 = C(13, 9) - 10 = 715 - 10 = 705.
%e A_NEW At d = 19: |E_19^(1)| = C(8+9, 9) - 10 = C(17, 9) - 10 = 24310 - 10 = 24300.
%K A_NEW nonn,easy
%O A_NEW 7,1
%A A_NEW Clay Elmore, [DATE]
```

## %S — Sequence B_NEW (one term per k, unpaired)

The deeper combinatorial sequence — the multiset coefficient minus 10. This
is the more natural form for OEIS since it has no pairing artifact.

```
%I B_NEW
%S B_NEW 45,210,705,1992,4995,11430,24300,48610,92368,167950,293920,497410,817180,1307494
%N B_NEW C(k+9, 9) - 10: the number of multisets of size k from a 10-letter
alphabet that are not repdigits, for k >= 2.
%C B_NEW C(k+9, 9) counts multisets of size k drawn from {0,1,...,9}; ten of
these are repdigits (one per digit value), so a(k) = C(k+9, 9) - 10 counts
the non-repdigit multisets.
%C B_NEW Arises in C. Elmore (2026) as |E_d^(1)|, the size of the step-1 escape
class for the 60714 lifting at digit length d, where k = floor((d-3)/2). See
A_NEW for the paired (odd-ladder, even-ladder) form.
%F B_NEW a(k) = C(k+9, 9) - 10 = (k+9)!/(9! k!) - 10 for k >= 2.
%H B_NEW C. Elmore, <a href="https://arxiv.org/abs/PENDING">Universal Full-Variable
Fixed Points Under Coefficient-Preserving Liftings</a>, arXiv preprint, 2026.
%e B_NEW At k=2: C(11, 9) - 10 = 55 - 10 = 45.
%e B_NEW At k=3: C(12, 9) - 10 = 220 - 10 = 210.
%e B_NEW At k=4: C(13, 9) - 10 = 715 - 10 = 705.
%e B_NEW At k=8: C(17, 9) - 10 = 24310 - 10 = 24300.
%K B_NEW nonn,easy
%O B_NEW 2,1
%A B_NEW Clay Elmore, [DATE]
```

## Process note

The previous version of this file had:

  binomial argument).
- Hand-computed terms that diverged from the formula at d=19, 20 (24165

draft). The pattern: derive at the base case, generalize without verifying,
miss the off-by-one until external review.

The remediation in this file: every numerical claim above was generated by
executing Python against the formula, not transcribed from memory or earlier
drafts. Future numerical claims in any externally-facing artifact should
follow the same rule.
